A Little About Me

I've always been a tinkerer at heart, the kid who would dismantle toys just to see how they worked, then rebuild them into something wildly impractical but fun. That curiosity never left; it just evolved. Now, instead of toys, I take apart pixels and workflows, asking, "How can this feel more alive?"

My path to design wasn't a straight line. I studied maps and landscapes in university, fascinated by how they quietly guide us through the world. That obsession with clarity and connection stuck with me. When I stumbled into UI/UX, it clicked: digital spaces are just another kind of map. Every button, every transition, is a signpost helping someone find their way.

Along the way, I've learned that the best designs start with stories, not wireframes. Like the time I stayed up sketching a feature for a healthcare app, only to realize halfway through that what users truly needed wasn't flashy tech, but simplicity. Or the thrill of watching a shy junior designer on my team pitch an idea that lit up the room.

When I'm not designing, I'm probably brewing overly elaborate coffee, experimenting with AI tools to see their "personality," or scribbling app ideas on napkins. Most are terrible, but every now and then, there's gold.

I'm drawn to the messy, human side of tech, the gaps where empathy meets innovation. Whether I'm mentoring, brainstorming, or geeking out over a slick interaction, I'm happiest when solving puzzles that make life a little kinder and smoother.
